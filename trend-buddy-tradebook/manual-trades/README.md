# Personal Notes

## How do I determine who is in control of price?
* Using bar by bar analysis on the 1-min, 5-min and 60-min charts
  * Looking for signs that a stock is trending
  * Looking for signs that a pullback is "healthy"
    * The pullback isn't overly deep
    * The pullback bounces before breaking below the previous range/level/MAs
    * The pullback doesn't have dramatic single candle moves against the trend
  * Candle patterns that signal strength / weakness
* Watching how the candle is formed and looking for signs that price action is struggling at a specific price.

## My definition of chop:
* Range bound price action, where price does not have clear directionality and will reverse with little or no warning.

## How do I try to avoid trading in chop
* I usually close trades early, which helps avoid getting caught in an unexpected reversal
* I also try avoid taking trades within tight ranges, and instead play the breakout from the ranges
  * I usually have a hard time seeing wider ranges, so I'm more likely to get caught up in the chop of those range.
  * I also have a hard time telling if the initial move bouncing off the far end of a range is a reversal or the start of a range.
